In the analysis, a loose event-based bad-jet veto is applied. It was found that this cut has a different efficiency in data (0.1\%) and MC (0.01\%) and that the observed differences are constant/flat. This can be seen (for data and the main background processes) in Figures \ref{fig:jetcleaning_inclusive} for an inclusive selection, in Figures \ref{fig:jetcleaning_0b} for a selection without $b$-jets and in Figures \ref{fig:jetcleaning_1b}-\ref{fig:jetcleaning_1b_brapidity} for a selection with exactly one $b$-jet in the final state. These checks were performed exemplarily for the muon channel. The invariant dilepton mass range between $130\,\text{GeV}\leq m_{\ell\ell}\leq500\,\text{GeV}$ is considered for the plots. 
For now, we decided to apply the jet cleaning cut. In an EB meeting it was decided to rerun the analysis without the jet veto cut after unblinding to check potential changes for limits.
