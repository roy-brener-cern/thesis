A study was performed to check the impact of the $\mathrm{min}(m_{\ell b})>175\,\mathrm{GeV}$-cut on the resulting limits. Therefore, the limits extracted in SRs where just the $\metsig$-cut is applied are compared to the limits that are extracted in SRs where the $\metsig$-cut and the $\mathrm{min}(m_{\ell b})$-cut is applied. It should be noted that the latter cut does not affect the 0 $b$-jet SR and therefore, the limits are just compared for the combined fit of all SRs and for the individual fits of SRs containing $b$-jets.
The expected cross-section limits are shown in Figures \ref{fig:exp_limits_metsig} and \ref{fig:exp_limits_metsig_mlb} for the two aforementioned scenarios. The limit values are also compared in Tables \ref{tab:impact_mlb_mu_combined}-\ref{tab:impact_mlb_mu_atleast2b} for the muon channel and in Tables \ref{tab:impact_mlb_ele_combined}-\ref{tab:impact_mlb_ele_atleast2b} for the electron channel.
It can be seen that there is an improvement in the expected cross-section limits up to $m_{Z'}\approx 1.2\,\text{TeV}$ when applying the additional cut. The largest improvement can be seen in the fit of the $\geq$2 $b$-jets SR.
Based on these results, it was decided to use the $\mathrm{min}(m_{\ell b})$-cut for the analysis.
