After optimising the $\mathrm{min}(m_{\ell b})$-cut, it is also necessary to test different choices of $\metsig$-cuts in the limit setting and to optimise this cut as well.
Therefore, expected cross-section limits were estimated and compared for different choices of the $\metsig$-cut: A scenario without $\metsig$-cut and different (maximum) values of $\metsig$ between 2 and 7 were considered in this study ($\mathrm{min}(m_{\ell b})>155\,\mathrm{GeV}$ was considered in all of these scenarios). This was done for three different signal mass points ($m_{Z'}=500\,\mathrm{GeV}$, $m_{Z'}=700\,\mathrm{GeV}$ (and $m_{Z'}=1500\,\mathrm{GeV}$) with each two different coupling parameters). The results can be found in Figure \ref{fig:metsig_cut_opt_mu} for the muon channel and in Figure \ref{fig:metsig_cut_opt_ele} for the electron channel. The corresponding values of the expected cross-section limits are shown in Tables \ref{tab:metsig_cut_opt_mu05_comb}-\ref{tab:metsig_cut_opt_ele10_atleast2b}.
The impact of the choice of the cut is the largest for smaller $Z'$ boson masses. But it can be seen, that the best limits can be achieved with $\metsig<4$ or $\metsig<5$ in the muon channel, and with $\metsig<5$ or $\metsig<6$ in the electron channel.
Additionally, it needs to be notices that the $Z'$+MET analysis uses a $\metsig>5$-requirement in their signal region. In order to keep the signal regions of both analyses orthogonal, we should stick to $\metsig<5$ in our signal region definition.
Based on this, it was decided to use a cut of $\metsig<5$ in all signal regions.
