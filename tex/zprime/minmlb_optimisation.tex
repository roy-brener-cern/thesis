A study was performed in order to optimise the $\mathrm{min}(m_{\ell b})$-cut. For that purpose, expected cross-section limits were estimated and compared for different choices of the cut, namely for no $\mathrm{min}(m_{\ell b})$-cut and for thresholds between $140\,\mathrm{GeV}$ and $180\,\mathrm{GeV}$ (in steps of $5\,\mathrm{GeV}$). This was done for three different signal mass points ($m_{Z'}=500\,\mathrm{GeV}$, $m_{Z'}=700\,\mathrm{GeV}$ and $m_{Z'}=1500\,\mathrm{GeV}$) for the muon and electron channel (with each two coupling parameters $g$). The results can be found in Figure \ref{fig:cut_opt_mu} for the muon channel and in Figure \ref{fig:cut_opt_ele} for the electron channel. The corresponding values of the expected cross-section limits are shown in Tables \ref{tab:cut_opt_mu05_comb}- \ref{tab:cut_opt_ele10_atleast2b}.
It can be seen that the best expected cross-section limits can be achieved with $\mathrm{min}(m_{\ell b})$-threshold between $150\,\mathrm{GeV}$ and $160\,\mathrm{GeV}$ at low masses of the $Z'$ boson. For higher masses of the $Z'$ boson, the impact of the different cuts is very small or even negligible. Based on these findings, it was decided to use a cut of  $\mathrm{min}(m_{\ell b})>155\,\mathrm{GeV}$ in the signal regions cntaining $b$-jets.
